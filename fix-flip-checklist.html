The Fix & Flip Underwriting Checklist

The exact criteria SGC uses to evaluate fix and flip deals, applied across $500M+ in funded loans. Work through it on every deal before you make an offer.

01 ARV Calculation Worksheet

Your After-Repair Value drives the entire deal. Get this wrong and nothing else matters.

  • Pull 3–5 sold comps within 0.5 miles, closed in the last 90 days
  • Match comps on bed/bath count, square footage (±20%), and post-rehab condition
  • Adjust for differences: lot size, garage, pool, finished basement, level of updates
  • Cross-check using comp price-per-square-foot × subject square footage
  • Review active and pending listings — that's your competition at resale
  • Underwrite to the median comp, not the highest — stay conservative

02 Rehab Cost Estimator

Walk the property with a contractor before you commit a number. Estimate from the structure out.

  • Big-ticket first: roof, foundation, and structural — these kill deals
  • Mechanicals: HVAC, electrical panel/rewire, plumbing repipe
  • Kitchen (cabinets, counters, appliances) and all bathrooms
  • Flooring, interior/exterior paint, drywall, trim, and doors
  • Windows, siding, landscaping, and overall curb appeal
  • Soft costs: permits, dumpsters, utilities during rehab
  • Add a 10–15% contingency for what's hiding behind the walls

03 Deal Scoring Criteria — SGC's Internal Rubric

If a deal can't clear these thresholds, walk away. Discipline beats optimism.

  • 70% Rule: Max offer ≤ (ARV × 0.70) − rehab cost
  • Profit margin: Target net profit of 15–20% of ARV after all costs
  • Days on market: Comps selling under 60 days signal a liquid exit
  • Exit spread: ARV comfortably above all-in (purchase + rehab + holding + selling)
  • Neighborhood: Stable or appreciating, low crime, desirable school zone
  • Scope match: Rehab level fits your crew's experience and timeline

04 Common Underwriting Mistakes to Avoid

Every one of these has turned a "great deal" into a loss. Don't make them.

  • Using list prices or Zestimates instead of verified sold comps for ARV
  • Skipping the contractor walkthrough and underestimating rehab
  • Forgetting holding costs — loan interest, taxes, insurance, utilities
  • Ignoring selling costs: agent commissions, closing, buyer concessions
  • Running no contingency line for hidden damage and overruns
  • Stretching the ARV to justify a price you've already fallen for
